Methodology

How it works

Three steps from raw audio to a calibrated confidence score with feature attribution.

1

Record or Upload

Record 5–10 s of sustained vowel /aaa/ in the browser via Web Audio API + MediaRecorder, or upload an existing WAV/MP3.

2

Feature Extraction

63 acoustic biomarkers are computed: MFCCs (13 × 3 stats), F0 via YIN pitch tracking, jitter, shimmer, HNR, spectral centroid, rolloff, flatness, ZCR, and 12 chroma features.

3

ML Inference

Three calibrated Random Forest models score the sample in parallel: Parkinson's (LOOCV AUROC 0.989, Italian corpus), Respiratory / COVID-19 pattern (Coswara, AUROC 0.817), and Depression affect (RAVDESS proxy, AUROC 0.737). Feature importances reveal which biomarkers drove each prediction.

Acoustic Biomarkers

63 features, 4 categories

Each feature captures a different dimension of vocal motor control.

Vocal Quality

Jitter · Shimmer · HNR

Cycle-to-cycle pitch (jitter) and amplitude (shimmer) variations indicate reduced laryngeal motor control. Harmonics-to-noise ratio quantifies breathiness — all early Parkinson's markers.

Cepstral

MFCCs (13 × 3 statistics)

Mel-frequency cepstral coefficients capture the vocal tract transfer function. Mean, standard deviation, and delta statistics encode timbre and temporal dynamics in 39 scalar values.

Prosody

Fundamental Frequency F0

Mean, standard deviation, and range of pitch reflect monotonicity and reduced variability — characteristic of Parkinson's hypophonia, computed via YIN pitch tracking.

Spectral

Centroid · Rolloff · Chroma · ZCR

Spectral centroid and rolloff describe energy distribution. Zero-crossing rate and 12 chroma features capture harmonic structure differences in 16 additional scalar values.
